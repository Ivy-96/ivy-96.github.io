About Me

I am a biotechnologist, biomedical engineer, writer,
editor, and spoken word poet dedicated to the
mission of integrating science and art.

Upon viewing the world as a student of biology
for over seven years and as a writer and
performance artist for over five years, I have
discovered a liberating synergy at the intersection
of science and art. To me, this synergy is most
apparent in the field of cognitive neuroscience. As a strong proponent of interdisciplinarity in research, I believe cognitive science can inform language (broadly,
all art) to maximize the desired impact of a specific artistic piece. On the other hand, it is already established that language and other naturalistic conditions can be used as tools for cognitive engineering to generate brain states that are otherwise difficult to achieve in typical laboratory settings.

Having had the opportunity to present at scientific conferences as well as at literature festivals, I am tired of clich√©s enforcing the chasm between art and science where young scientists view the arts as lacking rigor, and young artists view science as lacking imagination.

I want to help create a world where science and art are no longer perceived as separate entities, but instead established as two meaningful lines of work that reinforce each other and provide a solid platform for people from a diverse range of backgrounds to connect simply by virtue of some degree of similarity in human cognitive processing.
